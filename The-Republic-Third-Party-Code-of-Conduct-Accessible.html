Global Supplier Standards / Version 1.4

Third Party Code of Conduct

Integrity, human rights, and compliance expectations for our partners, suppliers, and service providers worldwide.

A Message to Our Third Parties

The Republic is committed to conducting business with the highest standards of ethics, transparency, and respect for human rights. We expect every third party that does business with us, or acts on our behalf, to share this commitment and to strictly adhere to all applicable laws and regulations wherever they operate. This Code reflects the values that define The Republic and translates them into concrete, day-to-day expectations for our global partner ecosystem.

This Code applies to all third parties doing business with or acting on behalf of The Republic, including but not limited to service providers, suppliers, vendors, alliance partners, consultants, agents, and contractors. The expectations described in this Code apply to all employees, other personnel, and any other parties engaged by our third parties in the course of their dealings with The Republic. Third parties are responsible for ensuring that their own personnel, subcontractors, and downstream suppliers understand and comply with these expectations.

This Code provides guidance for managing some of the risks third parties might face, but it cannot cover every potential situation. It is therefore vitally important that third parties be prepared to raise issues and ask questions as necessary. Violations of this Code may result in termination of services, subject to any relevant contractual provisions, and The Republic may exercise any other rights it has with respect to any violations. This Code is subject to regular review by The Republic.

Speaking Up — How to Raise a Concern

Third parties should provide appropriate resources that allow their personnel to ask questions and report concerns. Third parties should also maintain a clear non-retaliation policy that protects those who report concerns in good faith. Third parties may direct any questions or concerns to contact@republic.global for general, ethics, legal, or compliance matters, or to privacy@republic.global for data protection and information security concerns. Additional information is available at republic.global.

Section 01

Compliance with Laws and Regulations

The Republic expects third parties to comply with all applicable laws, rules, and policies in every jurisdiction in which they operate. Third parties must also possess all necessary licenses and permits applicable to the services and goods they provide to The Republic. Compliance with the letter and spirit of the law is the foundation on which all other expectations in this Code rest.

Avoiding Bribery and Corruption

Third parties must not bribe or accept bribes or engage in other corrupt conduct. Bribes can be anything of value, including cash, meant to influence decision-making or secure a business advantage. The Republic expects its third parties to conduct business transparently and to never seek a business advantage through bribery or corruption.

Third parties should take care when working with government officials on behalf of The Republic and must never provide payments to expedite routine government actions (such as approving permits) unless such services are offered publicly and payments are made through official means. Third parties are also responsible for making sure their own third parties, employees, agents, business partners, and contractors comply with these expectations.

Competing Fairly

Third parties are expected to comply with all applicable competition laws, to avoid seeking any unfair advantage over any competitors, and to use only legal methods to gather information about the market. Third parties should act transparently with all partners, clients, competitors, and other third parties. Third parties must avoid any discussions with competitors about agreements to set prices or allocate markets.

Complying with Trade Laws

Third parties are expected to comply with all applicable economic and trade sanctions or trade embargoes. Third parties should take care not to conduct business with individuals or entities prohibited by applicable sanctions, and must screen counterparties accordingly. Where The Republic provides specific screening instructions or restricted-party lists, third parties must apply them in a timely and documented manner.

Complying with Tax Laws

Third parties are expected to comply with all applicable tax laws and regulations. Third parties shall not facilitate any tax evasion by others and shall maintain reasonable measures to prevent the criminal facilitation of tax evasion by their employees or other associated third parties. The Republic will not tolerate any tax evasion or tax fraud committed by our third parties, and reserves the right to report suspected offences to the relevant authorities.

Anti-Money Laundering

The Republic expects third parties to comply with applicable laws related to money laundering or the funding of terrorist activities. Third parties must identify and report any suspicious signs of either money laundering (the process of trying to hide the illegal source of income) or terrorist financing. Suspicious signs may include business partners providing vague or fictitious information; requesting unusual or significant changes in transactions, such as a change in the frequency, type, and location of payment; or making any other out-of-the-ordinary or suspicious requests regarding transactions.

Section 02

Working Honestly, Transparently, and Safely

Navigating Conflicts of Interest

Conflicts can arise when a third party’s interests interfere, or appear to interfere, with The Republic’s interests. Third parties should avoid any conflict of interest or even the appearance of impropriety. Third parties must not:

  • Use The Republic’s resources, influence, or name to promote themselves without The Republic’s specific consent.
  • Use The Republic’s resources or information for an outside business or opportunity.
  • Pursue or encourage others to pursue business opportunities learned from their relationship with The Republic or that otherwise belong to The Republic.

Third parties must disclose any potential conflicts to The Republic as soon as they are recognized. The Republic discourages gifts to its employees from third parties, and third parties should expect that a Republic employee may return a gift that does not meet The Republic’s gift guidelines. The Republic prohibits any third party from making a charitable donation on its behalf.

Using The Republic’s Property

Third parties must protect and use The Republic’s property only as directed. Republic property includes any proprietary information, facilities, equipment, software, and email, as well as the Republic name and the goodwill associated with our brand. Republic property may not be used for purposes outside the scope of the engagement without prior written authorization.

Protecting Intellectual Property

Third parties are expected to protect The Republic’s intellectual property (IP) and to use IP only as directed. Third parties may not share Republic IP with others without express consent to do so. The Republic’s IP includes any copyright, trademark, patent, trade secret, or other confidential technical material, as well as any other intellectual property associated rights. Use of The Republic’s name, logo, and other brand materials is allowed only with prior written consent.

Communications

In general, third parties may not speak for The Republic. When they make public statements or conduct other business communications, third parties must make sure there is no confusion about who is making the statements, and they should not speak for The Republic unless specifically authorized to do so. Any media or analyst inquiry received by a third party in connection with its Republic engagement must be referred to The Republic without response.

Working with The Republic’s Clients

Third parties must respect and observe any Republic client instructions, such as policies, procedures, and guidelines, that apply to their work. Third parties must not engage any subcontractor in connection with a Republic client engagement without The Republic’s prior written consent, and remain fully responsible for the acts and omissions of any subcontractor they are permitted to engage.

Keeping Records

Third parties must keep accurate and complete records, must never alter or falsify records in the course of doing business with The Republic, and must retain records as agreed by contract and subject to applicable law. Complete and accurate records must be provided promptly when requested, should The Republic conduct an inspection of documents or other review. Third parties must have appropriate processes in place to avoid fraud, waste, and abuse.

Interacting with the Government

Third parties interacting with government entities on behalf of The Republic must do so with candor and diligence. Third parties must cooperate with any reasonable governmental requests and inform The Republic, to the extent permitted by applicable law, of any governmental requests related to our mutual business. Third parties must not, on The Republic’s behalf, make any statement or commitment to a government official that they are not authorized to make.

Avoiding Insider Trading

Third parties, including their personnel, should not trade stock or other securities based on any material, non-public information learned from their business relationship with The Republic, nor should they tip others who might trade on such information. Material non-public information may include, among other things, unpublished financial results, pending mergers or acquisitions, significant personnel changes, or major client wins or losses.

Protecting Privacy and Data

Third parties must maintain the confidentiality of all Republic and Republic-client confidential information and data, and must use it only for its intended purposes. The Republic expects third parties to observe the applicable data privacy laws and regulations and to process personal data on behalf of The Republic only as permitted by documented instructions from The Republic.

Third parties who have access to personal data must ensure that the appropriate physical, administrative, technical, and organizational measures are in place to protect it. Third parties must also comply with applicable standards in maintaining the security and privacy of any data and information systems used to conduct business with The Republic or its clients. Third parties must ensure that adequate measures are in place to prevent, detect, and respond to any data security breach, and must immediately report any data or privacy breach to The Republic by email to privacy@republic.global.

Using Artificial Intelligence Responsibly

Third parties must disclose to The Republic when artificial intelligence (AI) will be used in an engagement and comply with The Republic’s instructions and contractual limitations. Third parties must use AI responsibly in their work for and with The Republic and comply with applicable laws, including intellectual property laws, data protection laws, and any sector-specific AI regulations. Third parties must maintain appropriate human oversight of AI systems, ensure that outputs are validated for accuracy and bias, and must not input Republic confidential or personal data into any AI system without The Republic’s prior written consent.

Section 03

Supporting Our Environment

Third parties should respect the environment in the communities where they conduct business and must follow all applicable laws and regulations relating to the environment. Third parties should pay special attention to the conservation of natural resources, including water and energy, and should integrate recycling of materials into routine business practices. Where feasible, third parties are encouraged to adopt science-based emissions reduction targets, to track and report their environmental performance, and to collaborate with The Republic on shared sustainability objectives.

Section 04

Ensuring Respectful Working Conditions

Showing Respect

Third parties should embrace fair employment practices and prohibit all forms of discrimination, including those based on race, color, ethnicity, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, nationality, disability, age, or any other trait protected by applicable law. Third parties should also discourage any form of harassment in the workplace. Harassment is any kind of unwelcome speech or physical conduct, such as making offensive comments or gestures, or subjecting others to bullying, unwanted touching, or retaliation. It also includes sexual harassment, such as engaging in unwanted physical contact; making unwelcome remarks, jokes, or advances; demanding sexual favors; or engaging in other unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature.

The Republic encourages third parties to support inclusive working environments for all employees, personnel, and other third parties, and to provide accessible mechanisms for raising and resolving workplace concerns.

Working Safely

Third parties must maintain a safe working environment free of unsafe conditions and unsafe behaviors. Third parties must comply with all applicable health and safety laws and regulations, provide appropriate training and personal protective equipment, and maintain documented procedures for incident reporting and investigation. Workers must not be required to perform tasks under conditions that pose an unreasonable risk to their physical or mental health.

Section 05

Supporting Human Rights

The Republic is committed to internationally recognized human rights, and our third parties should share that commitment and promote the health and wellbeing of all employees. This commitment is grounded in the principles set forth in the United Nations Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights, the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, and the International Labor Organization Declaration on Fundamental Principles and Rights at Work. If faced with conflicting requirements, The Republic seeks to honor the principles of internationally recognized human rights.

Third parties must observe all applicable labor and employment laws, including all laws regarding minimum wage, minimum age requirements for employment, workers’ compensation, workplace conditions, and other laws regarding the health and safety of employees. Third parties must also comply with all applicable laws and regulations regarding the use of forced labor, slavery, human trafficking, and child labor, and must guard against the existence of any such illegal labor practices in every part of their supply chains.

Republic Standards for Third Parties

As a condition of doing business with The Republic, every third party is required to understand and comply with the following standards, which reflect the core human-rights principles that govern our own operations. The seven standards are listed in the table below.

Republic Standards for Third Parties — seven numbered standards describing required behavior on forced labor, discrimination, respect, freedom of association, workplace safety, supply chain diligence, and resource use.
Number Standard What This Means for Third Parties
1 Reject forced labor and human trafficking. Do not engage directly or indirectly in any use of forced labor, child labor, or trafficking in persons. Every worker must be employed voluntarily under terms they freely accept. Third parties must report any known or reasonably suspected human trafficking to the appropriate authorities and to The Republic promptly.
2 Make fair and merit-based employment decisions. Do not tolerate discrimination against any person for legally protected characteristics such as race, color, ethnicity, religion, sex, sexual orientation or gender expression, age, nationality, or disability. Every worker is entitled to fair compensation and working conditions consistent with applicable law.
3 Treat others with respect. Engage with co-workers, clients, and other parties with professional courtesy, dignity, and respect. Every person has the right to work in an environment free of harassment, bullying, or abuse of authority.
4 Respect freedom of association. Respect the right of all workers to interact and organize among themselves to collectively pursue common interests, including the right to join, or not join, an association of their choosing, to peacefully assemble, and to bargain collectively in accordance with applicable law.
5 Maintain a safe and healthy workplace. Follow applicable health and safety laws and regulations, as well as The Republic’s policies related to safe and healthy workplaces. Raise any potential health or safety issues to Republic management promptly.
6 Engage downstream third parties with care. The Republic has zero tolerance for human-rights abuses in its supply chain. Third parties must cascade equivalent expectations to their own suppliers and subcontractors and must take corrective action, including termination, where abuses are identified.
7 Use resources thoughtfully. Support the reduction of environmental impact through energy management, water stewardship, and waste reduction, in line with The Republic’s sustainability commitments.

Implementation and Monitoring

The Republic implements its commitment to human rights through policies and procedures, a due-diligence process to identify, prevent, and mitigate negative impacts on human rights, and processes to enable the company to remediate any adverse human-rights impacts to which it may contribute. The Republic performs human-rights due diligence using a variety of methods, including risk assessments (such as supplier risk assessments) and the application of established frameworks to new services (such as frameworks for responsible artificial intelligence).

The Republic expects its third parties to maintain equivalent due-diligence processes within their own operations and supply chains, to keep records of their assessments, and to make those records available to The Republic on request. Third parties must promptly remediate any adverse human-rights impacts identified in connection with their work for The Republic.

Grievance Mechanisms

The Republic is committed to supporting the identification of adverse human-rights impacts that it may cause or to which it may contribute, to addressing any such grievances promptly and consistently, and to remediating any adverse impacts. In support of this commitment, The Republic makes available channels through which any individual may raise concerns related to ethics or human rights, including the email addresses listed in this Code. Third parties are expected to establish equivalent grievance mechanisms for their own personnel and supply chains, and to communicate the existence of those mechanisms to their workforce in a language and format they understand.

Section 06

Consequences of Non-Compliance

Any third party, regardless of size or strategic importance, that violates any provision of this Code may be subject to measures up to and including termination of the business relationship and removal from The Republic’s approved-vendor list. Violations by individuals engaged by a third party may result in the third party being required to remove those individuals from the Republic engagement. The Republic may also invoke contractual remedies, seek alternative partners, and where required by law, report the matter to the relevant authorities.

Reporting and Non-Retaliation

The Republic encourages individuals to speak up without fear of retaliation when they see or suspect violations of this Code. Retaliation against any person who, in good faith, reports a concern or participates in an investigation is strictly prohibited and will not be tolerated. Third parties must maintain their own non-retaliation policies and must communicate those policies to all personnel engaged in Republic work.
